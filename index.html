Introduction

Waste accumulation is a primary global concern, rising rapidly with population growth and industrialization.

Current disposal methods involve uncontrolled dumping which is injurious to human health and the environment.

"Efficient waste management techniques can be accomplished using smart waste bins."
3.4B Tonnes of MSW annually by 2050
4634 Tons/Day in major cities

Statement of the Problem

Resource Management Challenge

Hospitality industries struggle with efficient resource management, particularly waste, water, and energy.

Sustainability Gap

Poor adherence to SDG goals affects brand reputation.

Customer Retention

Eco-conscious guests avoid non-sustainable hotels.

Operational Cost

Inefficient manual collection wastes labor and fuel.

The Research Gap

  • Global Context: Existing IoT solutions often fail in developing countries due to socioeconomic factors and infrastructure.
  • Hospitality Focus: Current studies lack holistic strategies for customer retention through waste management.
  • Food Waste: Insufficient exploration of customer food wastage behaviors in hospitality.

Aim of the Study

To develop a web-based system that incubates smart-waste bins across hospitality industries that tracks waste level, sends corresponding data to web-based service, and connects these industries to garbage collection services.

Goal: Enhance Customer Retention via Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

Hardware Components

  • Ultrasonic Sensor (HC-SR04)
    Measures distance to waste surface to determine fill level.
  • Load Cell + HX711
    Measures the physical weight of the waste.
  • Microcontroller (Arduino/ESP8266)
    Processes sensor data and sends it to the cloud via Wi-Fi.
